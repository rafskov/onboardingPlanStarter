# Outsourced AE Operational Blueprint

## Value Proposition

- Technical founders and less-technical sellers need help getting in front of
  technical buyers to go from beta to market validation (0 → 5 customers).
- Some teams with one AE need help cracking larger accounts but do not have
  enough inbound to justify another full-time hire.
- Some founders have early product–market fit, are raising a new round, and
  need a fractional Head of Sales–type partner.

## Core Deliverables

- Structured product feedback from real customer conversations
- Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) and compelling events identified
- Closed-won deals or equivalent commercial milestones

## Intake and Fit Assessment

This checklist helps determine whether a prospect fits into a staggered,
multi-client portfolio, needs a full-time hire, or is a fit for a
contract-to-hire 90‑day engagement. 

Use these questions during discovery to pressure-test their requirements
against your **operational guardrails**.

### Fractional vs Full-Time Screening Checklist

1. **Lead triage ownership**  
   - Question:  
     “With significant PLG users and inbound, who is responsible for filtering
     ‘one-off’ users versus high-value enterprise targets?”  
   - Interpretation: If you are expected to manually vet the entire stream,
     it likely requires a full-time role.

2. **Conversion scope**  
   - Question:  
     “Are you looking for me to manage the entire PLG-to-paid funnel, or focus
     on larger accounts where enterprise features are the main drivers? Are you
     expecting a few plays executed or ownership of all GTM ahead of a hiring
     spree?”

3. **Technical proof ownership**  
   - Question:  
     “Given your typical sales cycle, what is the expectation for technical
     validation? Will I coordinate with an engineer, or own setup and basic
     troubleshooting for POCs and integrations?”

4. **Sales tech and RevOps**  
   - Question:  
     “Do you already have a stack (CRM, enrichment, call recording) I can plug
     into, or am I expected to build and maintain the sales engine from
     scratch?”

5. **Post‑sale handoff**  
   - Question:  
     “Once a contract is signed, does the account transition to a technical
     owner for implementation, or does the AE manage onboarding and initial
     configuration? Am I the main bridge between enterprise customers and the
     team for feature priority?”

6. **Founder time and meeting load**  
   - Question:  
     “Outside of a weekly 1–2 hour sales sync, what is the expectation for my
     participation in staff meetings, product sessions, or marketing strategy?”  
   - Interpretation: Your agreement caps this at ~2 hours/week; if they expect
     5+ hours/week, they are looking for an employee.

7. **Collateral production**  
   - Question:  
     “Do you already have core sales assets (demo environment, deck, security
     responses), or do you need me to spend 30+ hours in Month 1 building
     them?”

8. **Fundraising sprint**  
   - Question:  
     “If you are raising soon, is my primary goal to deliver raw revenue (logos)
     or to document a repeatable sales playbook you can hand to a future AE
     team? How involved do you expect me to be in the raise process?”

9. **Geography and events**  
   - Question:  
     “How critical is in‑person prospecting at industry events or founder
     meetups versus a primarily virtual outbound motion?”

10. **Target volume and quota**  
    - Question:  
      “To reach your next milestone, how many active discovery calls and
      proposal‑stage deals do you expect one seller to manage? How many demos,
      pilots, and discovery calls per week is realistic?”

11. **Marketing expectations**  
    - Question:  
      “Are you expecting help with ongoing content, community management, or
      broader marketing programs?”

### Engagement Decision Matrix

| Outcome          | Indicator                                                                 |
| :--------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Fractional AE    | Focus on enterprise; founder handles PLG triage; clear engineering SLA.   |
| Full‑time hire   | Needs player‑coach to build the org; AM/support duties; 20+ hrs/week.     |

**Rule of thumb:** If they need hiring support, content/SEO ownership, or
broad GTM leadership, that’s typically out of scope for a fractional AE and
points toward a full‑time role.

## Product and Market Fit Checks

### Technical Fit

- Can a buyer see the “aha” moment in 30 seconds or less? Is it easily demo‑able?
- Can one person configure a basic environment in 15 minutes or less (and how
  does that change for larger customers)?
- Is there deployment flexibility (e.g., sandbox, cloud, on‑prem options)?
- Are there meaningful data privacy or security concerns?
- Can the customer control their own costs and usage?
- What does performance look like from a developer’s point of view?
- Is model or system latency a problem?
- What happens when usage spikes?
- Can you explain what it does in 30 seconds or less?
- Is the architecture resilient to infrastructure or model pricing changes?

### Business Fit

- Does it solve a “hair‑on‑fire” problem?
- Does someone with budget authority care about this problem?
- Is this a budgeted line item or a new spend category?
- Is pricing consumption‑based, subscription (MRR), or hybrid?

### Sales Readiness

- Do they have any sales tech stack in place?
- Any closed‑won deals yet?
- Any lost deals and documented reasons?
- Is there any existing lead flow?
- Are they looking for meetings only, or closed‑won deals and revenue?
- Do they already have a deck, collateral, warmed‑up inboxes, tech stack, and
  demo environment set up?

## Out‑of‑Scope Work

- One‑to‑many content and media production
- Advanced RevOps and GTM system build‑outs (complex automation tools, etc.)
- Paid search, SEO, multi‑channel campaigns, webinars, and event marketing

These can be referred out or explicitly scoped in a separate agreement if
needed.

## Pricing Structure

- One‑time implementation fee
- Fixed monthly retainer plus commissions
- Optional equity component after 90 days or first closed sale

---

## Risk Management

- Founder agrees to SLAs for:
  - Slack or async queries
  - Customer‑facing technical deep‑dive questions
  - Demos and POCs  
  Including beta customer terms (especially data/privacy) to clearly define when
  a sale is valid.

- Leads and CRM records remain with the customer; your internal code, automations,
  and playbooks do not.

- Your IP (custom per engagement):
  - Demo script and environment
  - Sales deck
  - Discovery guide
  - Objection handling notes
  - One‑pager with battlecard
  - Core narrative (“mantra”) and outbound messaging

- Sales tech stack for lead/opp tracking, recording calls, and sequencing remains
  in place at the client but underlying configuration IP is yours.

---

## Contract Considerations

- The agreement should explicitly cover data transfer at the end of the
  engagement (CRM, notes, recordings, documents).
- Reference a standard services agreement template with:
  - Scope and out‑of‑scope
  - IP ownership
  - Termination and transition
  - SLAs and responsibilities

## 30‑60‑90 Outline (Seed, Pre‑AE Teams)

For seed‑stage teams where founders still own most selling.

### Days 0–30

- **Onboarding (Week 1–2)**
  - Value proposition refined and agreed
  - Demo script, deck, discovery guide, and product one‑pager drafted
  - Sales tech stack set up and connected
  - Roadmap and release feedback loop established (lightweight PM cadence)
  - Relationship mapping: investors, founders, and key advisors

- **Market Motion**
  - Target account list delivered
  - Messaging experiments created and deployed
  - At least 3 in‑person meetings scheduled (where geography allows)
  - Cold call list created and actively worked

### Days 31–60 (High‑Level)

- Formalize repeatable motion from discovery to proposal
- Start documenting objections, win/loss themes, and pricing feedback
- Push strongest early opportunities toward pilot or proposal stage

*(You can expand this section as you refine the exact 60–90 day motion.)*

## Budgeted Hours for IP Creation

Approximate one‑time effort per new client:

- **Onboarding (10 hours)**
  - Reviewing calls, demos, and product docs

- **Demo script and environment (20 hours)**
  - Environment setup
  - Recording a baseline demo
  - Iteration based on feedback

- **Sales deck (10 hours)**
  - ICP research
  - Market pain confirmation
  - Narrative (“mantra”) development
  - Battlecard/documentation

- **Discovery guide (5 hours)**
  - Internal interviews
  - Early customer calls

- **Product one‑pager (1 hour)**

- **Tech stack setup (10 hours)**
  - Cold email / outreach tools
  - Prospecting/intent tools
  - Call recording
  - CRM configuration

---

## Weekly Budgeted Hours (Ongoing Per Client)

- Find prospects for outbound (0.5 hrs)  
  - Configure “customer pain” or downtime intent signals
- Build messaging experiments (0.5 hrs)
- Deploy messaging (0.5 hrs)  
  - Includes outreach to investors or partners when relevant
- Cold calls (1 hr)
- In‑person drop‑ins and local events (5 hrs, if local model is in scope)
- Collateral and demo environment tuning (2.5 hrs)

Total: approximately 10 hours/week of planned time.

---

## Weekly Unbudgeted / Flexible Work

- Social posts for personal and client brand building
- Research and plan relevant events
- Plan walk route for local prospecting
- Purchase and prep any in‑person meeting materials (e.g., leave‑behinds)
