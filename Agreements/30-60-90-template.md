# Outsourced AE 30/60/90 Day Plan

## Three-Month Engagement, Contract-to-Hire

**Primary objective:** Build a repeatable early enterprise sales motion for the client, create
visible pipeline for the next financing milestone, and determine by Day 90 whether the right
next step is a full-time AE or Head of Sales role.

This plan uses a phased 30/60/90 structure: each phase separates AE responsibilities, client
responsibilities, and success metrics. It is adapted for early-stage B2B companies selling to
enterprise buyers with inbound signal, open-source usage, or founder network traction.

---

## Operating Model

### Working Hours and Coverage

- **Default working hours:** Monday through Friday, 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM local.
- **Customer calls and events:** Prospect meetings, technical validation sessions, and industry
  events may occur outside standard hours when required by prospect availability or event timing.
- **Weekly client sync:** One standing weekly meeting with client leadership to review pipeline,
  customer feedback, outbound results, pricing questions, technical blockers, and next-week
  priorities.
- **Async updates:** CRM notes and pipeline updates are the source of truth. Slack and email are
  used for fast coordination, not as the primary system of record.

### CRM and Sales System Expectations

- **CRM as source of truth:** All leads, accounts, contacts, opportunities, call summaries, next
  steps, deal stages, blockers, and follow-up dates will be captured in the client's CRM.
- **Call recording and notes:** Customer calls should be recorded and summarized through the
  client's preferred tooling.
- **Pipeline hygiene:** Every active opportunity should have an owner, stage, next step,
  next-step date, buyer role, technical status, commercial status, and current blocker.
- **Activity visibility:** Outbound sequences, inbound lead triage, follow-ups, proposal-stage
  opportunities, and customer feedback should be visible to client leadership without relying on
  ad hoc status requests.

### Scope of the AE Role

- **In scope:** Enterprise prospecting, inbound triage, discovery, qualification, customer calls,
  technical validation coordination, proposal management, pricing feedback, objections
  cataloging, CRM management, customer advocacy, and feedback loops to product and engineering.
- **Shared with client / engineering:** Technical proof design, integration troubleshooting,
  roadmap tradeoff decisions, discount approval, security and procurement responses, and product
  commitments.
- **Out of scope unless separately agreed:** General product marketing ownership, long-term
  customer success ownership, engineering troubleshooting, recruiting, finance operations, custom
  CRM integrations, and open-ended strategic work outside the operating cadence.

---

## Phase 1: Days 1–30

### Foundation, GTM Alignment, and CRM Control

**Primary goal:** Establish the sales operating system, inventory every active conversation,
define the first enterprise motion, and begin converting current inbound and network interest
into qualified customer calls.

### AE Responsibilities

- **CRM setup and cleanup:** Gain access to the client's CRM, define required fields, audit
  existing leads and accounts, and create a clean view of every active conversation, inbound
  lead, and open next step.
- **Conversation inventory:** Review prior customer conversations, founder notes, call
  recordings, inbound activity, and current product feedback to produce a single view of who
  has shown intent and what each account needs next.
- **ICP and signal definition:** Define the first target segment based on the buyer profile most
  likely to close within 90 days.
- **Sales narrative:** Convert founder knowledge into a structured point of view for discovery,
  including what pain the product solves, why now, which buyer owns the problem, and what
  commercial trigger matters.
- **Discovery guide and demo flow:** Build the initial discovery guide, qualification rubric,
  demo narrative, and technical validation path.
- **Inbound and network activation:** Triage existing inbound and investor/founder network
  leads, prioritize high-value targets, and route all follow-up through the CRM.
- **Outbound experiments:** Draft and launch the first messaging experiments against agreed
  account segments.
- **Customer calls:** Begin running customer and prospect calls, including calls outside default
  working hours when prospect availability or events require it.

### Client Responsibilities

- **Knowledge transfer:** Provide product walkthroughs, call recordings, pitch materials, prior
  customer notes, pricing context, buyer objections, and access to relevant product and
  engineering stakeholders.
- **CRM access:** Provide full access to the CRM and any call recording, sequencing,
  enrichment, or sales engagement tools required.
- **Lead context:** Share the current inbound list, usage signals, strategic accounts, investor
  introductions, and any active customer or prospect conversations.
- **Technical support path:** Identify the engineering or founder owner for technical questions,
  integration blockers, and validation calls.
- **Pricing and packaging input:** Clarify the current pricing hypothesis and how the product is
  packaged.
- **Weekly cadence:** Meet once per week to review pipeline, messaging, product feedback, and
  decisions needed to unblock deals.

### Success Metrics

- CRM has a clean initial pipeline with all known leads, accounts, contacts, opportunities, and
  next steps captured.
- First target account list and signal model are documented.
- Initial discovery guide, messaging experiments, and demo narrative are ready for use.
- At least 10 qualified customer or prospect calls are completed or scheduled with the AE
  running point.
- Client has a clear view of which conversations are real opportunities, which are
  education-only, and which need founder or engineering support.

## Phase 2: Days 31–60

### Market Testing and Pipeline Velocity

**Primary goal:** Move from setup to high-frequency selling, pressure-test the market narrative,
qualify active opportunities, and create proposal-stage pipeline.

### AE Responsibilities

- **Outbound execution:** Continue targeted outbound to enterprise accounts using the
  highest-performing messages from Phase 1, while killing weak experiments quickly.
- **Inbound conversion:** Work inbound, usage signal, investor-introduced, and founder-network
  leads through structured qualification.
- **Discovery and qualification:** Run customer calls that identify buyer pain, urgency,
  procurement path, technical validation needs, buying committee, budget, and next step.
- **POC control:** Coordinate technical validation with founders and engineering so the client
  avoids uncontrolled POCs and keeps each evaluation tied to a commercial next step.
- **Proposal motion:** Create a repeatable path from discovery to written quote, pilot, or
  commercial proposal.
- **Customer advocacy:** Capture customer feedback and translate it into product, packaging, and
  roadmap input.
- **CRM reporting:** Maintain a weekly pipeline view with deal stage, expected next step,
  blocker, forecast category, technical status, and founder action required.
- **Events and direct prospecting:** Attend relevant industry or customer events when they
  create qualified access, including outside standard hours when appropriate.

### Client Responsibilities

- **Technical SLA:** Respond to AE-submitted technical sales questions, validation blockers, and
  customer-specific product issues within an agreed turnaround window.
- **Pricing decisions:** Make timely calls on discounting, packaging, quote structure, and
  whether the motion for a given account is product-led or enterprise-led.
- **Founder participation:** Join strategic calls where founder credibility materially improves
  conversion.
- **Product feedback loop:** Review structured customer feedback weekly and decide which
  feedback becomes roadmap, sales positioning, or qualification criteria.
- **CRM discipline:** Keep founder-originated conversations and customer notes in the same CRM
  workflow so the AE has full pipeline visibility.

### Success Metrics

- A consistent weekly flow of qualified customer conversations is established.
- At least two opportunities move to proposal, pilot, quote, or equivalent commercial
  next-step stage.
- A structured validation or POC motion exists, including entry criteria, owner, success
  criteria, and commercial exit criteria.
- CRM reporting gives client leadership a reliable view of active pipeline, next steps,
  blockers, and forecast.
- Market objections, pricing feedback, buyer personas, and winning and losing messages are
  documented.

---

## Phase 3: Days 61–90

### Revenue Conversion and Contract-to-Hire Decision

**Primary goal:** Convert the strongest opportunities, document a repeatable sales motion, and
make a clear Day 90 decision on whether the role should convert to full-time.

### AE Responsibilities

- **Closing motion:** Manage qualified opportunities through proposal, negotiation, technical
  validation, procurement, and contract execution.
- **Deal strategy:** Identify which opportunities are closeable now, which need founder
  involvement, which require technical proof, and which should be disqualified.
- **Pricing refinement:** Use real customer feedback to recommend practical pricing, packaging,
  discounting, and quote structure for early enterprise deals.
- **Sales playbook:** Document the repeatable motion: ICP, triggers, messaging, discovery
  questions, demo flow, validation path, a process for receiving client referrals, CRM workflow,
  proposal process, and common objections.
- **Hiring recommendation:** Provide a contract-to-hire assessment stating whether the client
  needs a full-time AE, Head of Sales, founder-led sales with part-time support, or a different
  GTM motion.
- **Customer handoff:** For closed or late-stage opportunities, create clear handoff notes for
  implementation, customer success, technical support, and product follow-up.
- **CRM handoff:** Ensure all account history, notes, next steps, opportunity status, and deal
  context remain complete in the client's CRM.

### Client Responsibilities

- **Deal execution:** Provide timely support for legal, pricing approvals, product commitments,
  security and procurement answers, and executive-level customer communication.
- **Post-sale ownership:** Own implementation, support, product troubleshooting, and long-term
  customer success unless a separate owner is assigned.
- **Conversion decision:** Decide by Day 90 whether to convert the engagement to a full-time
  role, extend the outsourced AE arrangement, or shift to a different sales model.
- **Org design input:** Clarify whether the client is building toward founder-led sales, a first
  AE, Head of Sales, sales engineering, customer success, or a broader GTM hiring plan.

### Success Metrics

- One or more qualified opportunities reaches closed-won, signed pilot, paid evaluation, or a
  clearly defined late-stage commercial milestone.
- The active pipeline is fully documented in the CRM with next steps, blockers, forecast, and
  owner for every meaningful opportunity.
- Client has a repeatable sales playbook that can support a future AE, Head of Sales, or
  founder-led enterprise motion.
- Client leadership has a clear recommendation on whether to convert the role to full-time and
  what the next GTM hire should own.
- Customer feedback has been translated into a prioritized list of product, packaging, and
  sales-process recommendations.

---

## Decision Points to Confirm Before Start

- **Primary motion:** Should the client prioritize enterprise-led selling, product-led-to-enterprise
  conversion, founder-network selling, or a hybrid motion during the first 90 days?
- **ICP:** Which account segment matters most for the next financing milestone?
- **Technical proof:** What level of proof is required before a customer can buy, and who owns
  setup and troubleshooting?
- **Commercial threshold:** What qualifies as success by Day 90: closed revenue, paid pilots,
  proposal-stage pipeline, financing proof points, customer logos, or a documented repeatable
  motion?
- **CRM policy:** Which CRM fields, stages, call-recording process, and weekly reporting views
  should be mandatory from Day 1?
- **Hiring path:** If the engagement works, is the target role first AE, Head of Sales, GTM
  lead, or a hybrid title?
